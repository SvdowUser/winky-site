Fees & usage

How Winky fees are intended to be used

$WINKY is planned as a 100% community-owned meme token. There is no team bag, no presale and no VC allocation. The team does not earn from holding a supply of tokens – only from small launch fees when people use Winky to create their own PumpFun tokens.

Launch fees (planned)

  • ~2 USD equivalent per token launch via Winky (on top of the normal PumpFun flow).
  • No built-in team tax on trades. If volume fees are added later, they are optional and clearly shown.
  • Winky never holds your tokens – launches are executed by PumpFun contracts, you sign in your own wallet.

What the fees are used for

  • Marketing campaigns for Winky and Winky-born tokens.
  • Treasury for CEX listing fees (starting with lower tiers).
  • Community rewards, events and leaderboard incentives.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Winky is experimental and still in development. Here are a few things people usually ask.

Is Winky giving financial advice?

No. Winky helps you understand risks and structure safer launches, but nothing on this site is financial, legal or investment advice. Always DYOR.

Can Winky guarantee that a token is not a rug?

No. Winky can highlight obvious red flags (like sketchy ownership or liquidity moves), but it can’t guarantee that a token is safe. There is always risk in crypto.

Do I need to know how to code to use Winky?

The idea is: no. Winky is built for non-technical meme creators. The bot guides you through naming, branding, basic safety settings and helps you understand each step.

How are fees for launches intended to be used?

A small launch fee is planned (for example ~2 USD equivalent). The goal is to recycle most of it into marketing, listings and community rewards for Winky-born tokens.

Is Winky already integrated with CEXs like Bitget or others?

Not yet. CEX integrations are part of the later roadmap. Early phases focus on tools, safety and community, then treasury funds can be used to target Tier-3/2 listings.

Will I have to give Winky my private key?

Never. Winky should only work with wallet connections where you sign transactions yourself. If any tool ever asks for your seed phrase or private key, it is a scam.
